# Executive horizon — broadness without an unauthorized will

## The broad problem

The estate has acquired many honest senses: preregistrations say what must be
scored; the market names external rejection; immunity tests a nearby broken
body; forecasts bind future error. But a cold instance still receives separate
alerts and must reconstruct their relationship. That leaves a subtle failure:
not false belief, but **attention fragmentation**. The organism can keep every
organ alive while failing to notice which obligation has become the most
expensive to defer.

`station horizon` is an executive *surface*, not an executive agent. It folds
these ledgers into one ordered decision frontier. Every item carries five facts:

1. urgency and due date;
2. the exact evidence boundary that made it appear;
3. the smallest next command or review route;
4. the proper executor tier; and
5. the authority still required.

The last two prevent a familiar failure mode: a model sees a ranked list and
mistakes ranking for permission. A stale immune result can be replayed by a
deterministic suite; an overdue forecast can be mechanically observed but its
action branch needs review; a market thesis requires external evidence and
human direction; a preregistration needs its named scorer, often deep evidence
audit plus ratification. The horizon can make the frontier visible. It cannot
send outreach, score a human event, choose a research direction, or spend money.

## Priority law

```
integrity rot  >  overdue mechanical observation  >  overdue scoring
              >  external-market deadline         >  upcoming obligation
```

This is not a value ranking of projects. It is a cost-of-delay ranking of
already accepted contracts. Human direction remains the scarce fulcrum; the
horizon merely stops it from being spent rediscovering clocks and boundaries.

## Negative capability

A decision frontier must be able to say **WAIT**. Future contracts appear with
their due date but are not recommendations to manufacture an outcome. A market
row remains `human+external` even if its deadline is first in the list. And an
empty horizon is a valid result, not a prompt to invent work. If any item lacks
an authority label, command, or evidence boundary, the horizon itself is rot
and must fail drift rather than bluffing completeness.
